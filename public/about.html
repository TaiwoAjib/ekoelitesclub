ABOUT US

One Lagos family, seven thousand miles from home.

Ékò Ilé …Gba Be! — Eko Oni Baje. We are Lagosians in Indiana, bound by heritage and committed to each other.

OUR STORY

How it began
ADD images/history.jpg

Eko Elites Club Inc. was founded by a small circle of Lagos indigenes who found themselves far from the energy of Ékò — the markets of Idumota, the waters of the Lagoon, the sound of fuji at a Saturday owambe — and decided that distance would not dim their heritage.

What began as informal gatherings in living rooms across Indianapolis grew into a registered non-profit organization: a home away from home for sons and daughters of Lagos State, their families, and friends of Lagos across Indiana.

Today the club hosts cultural festivals, monthly general meetings, welfare programs, and community service projects — while sending support back to communities in Lagos State. Our rallying cry says it all: Eko Oni Baje — Lagos will not spoil.

OUR MISSION

To unite Lagos indigenes in Indiana, preserve and promote Ékò culture, and provide social, welfare, and economic support to our members and communities.

OUR VISION

A thriving, generational community where every Lagosian in Indiana finds family — and where Lagos itself feels our positive impact.

WHAT WE STAND FOR

Our values

Unity

One Lagos, one family — regardless of where in Ékò you call home.

Heritage

We pass Ékò language, festivals, and traditions to the next generation.

Service

We give back — to Indiana communities and to Lagos State.

Excellence

Elites in name and in conduct — we hold ourselves to a high standard.

MEMBERSHIP

Who can join?

Membership is open to adults of good character who identify with Lagos State — by birth, marriage, or genuine affinity — and who live in or around Indiana.
